<p><p>The near beer market consists of beverages containing low alcohol content, typically less than 0.5% ABV. It primarily offers two types: limit fermentation and dealcoholization methods. Limit fermentation involves restricting yeast activity during fermentation to produce low alcohol levels naturally. In contrast, the dealcoholization method removes alcohol from fully fermented beer, usually through techniques such as vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis. Both methods cater to consumers seeking flavorful alternatives to traditional beer while minimizing alcohol intake.</p></p>
